About

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist. He developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. Einstein's work is also known for its influence on the philosophy of science. Go here to learn more about Albert Einstein's life and work.

Major Achievements

  • Albert Einstein provided empirical evidence for the atomic theory.
  • He enabled the determination of Avogadro’s constant and therefore the size of molecules.
  • Einstein solved the riddle of the photoelectric effect.
  • He proposed the special theory of relativity.
  • Einstein came up with the concept of rest energy through his famous equation.
  • He proposed the general theory of relativity.
  • He collaborated with Bose to predict the existence of Bose–Einstein condensate.
  • His debates with Niels Bohr brought quantum mechanics in focus.
  • Albert Einstein was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921.
  • His work had profound and far reaching implications.
